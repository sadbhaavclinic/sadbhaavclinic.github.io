Disc Damage Likelyhood Scale (DDLS)

Disc Damage Likelihood Scale (DDLS)

The Disc Damage Likelihood Scale (DDLS) is useful for diagnosis, in grouping into categories of severity the damaged optic disc and in monitoring change.
 
DDLS Stage NW for Small Disc (<1.50 mm) NW for Average Size Disc (1.50-2.00 mm) NW for Large Disc (>2.00 mm) Functional damage
1 0.5 or more 0.4 or more 0.3 or more Not definitely damaged
2 0.4 to 0.49 0.3 to 0.39 0.2 to 0.29 Not definitely damaged
3 0.3 to 0.39 0.2 to 0.29 0.1 to 0.19 Not definitely damaged
4 0.2 to 0.29 0.1 to 0.19 Less than 0.1 Not definitely damaged
5 0.1 to 0.19 Less than 0.1 0 for less than 45° Asymptomatic glaucoma damage, minimal visual field defect or none
6 Less than 0.1 0 for less than 45° 0 for 46° to 90° Asymptomatic glaucoma damage, visual field defect always present
7 0 for less than 45° 0 for 46° to 90° 0 for 91° to 180° Asymptomatic glaucoma damage, moderate or more advanced visual field defect
8 0 for 46° to 90° 0 for 91° to 180° 0 for 181° to 270° Glaucomatous disease/disability
9 0 for 91° to 180° 0 for 181° to 270° 0 for more than 270° Glaucomatous disease/disability
10 0 for more than 180° 0 for more than 270° Glaucomatous disease/disability
*NW = Narrowest width of rim (rim/disc ratio) The location of rim narrowing is not considered but, especially in the early stages, it is important in differentiating a clear glaucomatous defect from a physiological aspect.
